We intend to optimize the reaction vertex in the SAMURAI dipole to make the best use of the coverage of the detectors. \textcolor{blue}{Here, $b$ is the impact parameter, defined as the perpendicular distance between the centers of the deuteron and target nucleus at closest approach, and $\rho$ is the nuclear matter density.} According to the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model calculations, for the scattering events with $b = 7 \sim 9$~fm, which is the range showing the most sensitivity of the IVR effect to the $\gamma$ parameter \textcolor{blue}{(where $\gamma$ characterizes the density dependence of the symmetry energy $E_{\rm sym}(\rho)$)}, the most probable direction of the breakup neutrons is not very close to $\theta = 0^\circ$. \textcolor{blue}{The SAMURAI spectrometer is a large-acceptance superconducting dipole magnet, providing high-resolution momentum analysis and wide angular coverage for charged particles.} Considering that NEBULA is difficult to move, in order to measure the neutrons in the most favorable range of $\theta_{\mathrm{n}}$, it is suggested to place the target upstream of the magnet, at a distance of 3 meters from the center of the magnet, which ensures that the range of $-10^\circ \leq \theta_{\mathrm{n}} \leq 10^\circ$ is covered for the most probable neutron emissions.
