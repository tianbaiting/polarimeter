Comment 1.6 (Page 5, line 53): "what's the '10%' from?"

Author response: The quoted "10%" refers to the relative uncertainty on the measured detector count ratios that are used to extract the tensor observables (pzz/p yy). Figures 6 and 7 contain large event samples so the pure statistical error is small; the ≈10% figure is the combined uncertainty from residual statistical contribution and systematic effects (detector efficiency, background subtraction and normalization). This level of uncertainty is sufficient to resolve pzz vs pyy at the stated precision. We have added a short explanation in the text and a clarifying note in the captions of Figs. 6 and 7.

Changes made:

Author response: 

Comment 1.9: Origins of "+/-12" and "30°":

Author response: The "+/-12" value is determined by the combination of the target position and the angular acceptance of the NEBULA detector and PDC. NEBULA's nominal angular acceptance is about ±10°, but by placing the target closer to NEBULA the effective coverage increases to roughly ±12°. And the position of PDC is designed to have nearly the same position ad NEBULA. A concise explanation has been added to the manuscript.

The "30°" spectrometer setting results from mechanical and entrance-window constraints of the SAMURAI magnet. Although SAMURAI can nominally rotate from −5° to 95°, the entrance-window geometry restricts practical positions (without replacing the window) to 0°, 30° and 90°. The 90° configuration blocks neutrons, while the 30° setting provides a larger proton angular acceptance than 0°, so we selected 30°. These are shown clearly in the note:
https://ribf.riken.jp/RIBF-TAC05/10_SAMURAI.pdf
